## Definition
A grounding comment is a `#` comment inside a concept snippet that states the
gotcha or actual runtime behavior (`# stop is exclusive`,
`# default evaluated once, at def time`). Every concept snippet carries at
least one; they carry the teaching, since prose is capped at ≤2 sentences.

## Why it matters / where it appears
It is the mechanism that keeps the workbook a workbook: explanation lives at
the exact line it applies to, not in paragraphs. tools/lint_workbook.py fails
any concept fence with no `#` comment. The size guardrail cuts concepts
before it ever shrinks grounding comments.

## Boundaries
Not a hint — hints appear in exercises and name a technique. Not a narration
comment ("# loop over items"); it must state a gotcha or real behavior worth
knowing.
