## Context
Dropping in the verbatim part-1.md exemplar, `tools/lint_workbook.py` flagged
it three ways: refactor exercises (1.2, 4.1) have no `...` placeholder; the
predict exercise (2.4) has no scaffold, `...`, or asserts; and the 1.3 scaffold
(`match cmd:` with a `...` body) is a genuine SyntaxError because the
placeholder occupies a grammar-required position. The exemplar is ground
truth, so the linter was wrong, not the file.

## Decision
Calibrate the linter to the exemplar: (1) the `...` and assert checks apply
only to function-building exercises; (2) assert-exempt exercises are detected
as no def/class scaffold + a rewrite/refactor/predict/convert instruction in
the exercise line or a comment; (3) exercise blocks containing `...` tolerate
SyntaxError — concept fences and `...`-free exercise blocks must still parse.

## Alternatives considered
- Edit part-1.md to satisfy the linter — rejected: the exemplar is verbatim
  and never edited (ANCHOR.md locked decision; its own instructions say
  FIX THE LINTER).
- Drop the parse check entirely — rejected: it still guards every concept
  snippet and any exercise block without a placeholder.

## Consequences
The linter now encodes the exemplar's real exercise taxonomy
(function-building / refactor / predict). Regression-checked against a
violation fixture: complete implementations, missing asserts, uncommented
concept fences, and broken concept snippets are all still caught.
